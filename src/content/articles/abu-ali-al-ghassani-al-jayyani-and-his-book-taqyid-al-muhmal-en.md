## Abu Ali al-Ghassani al-Jayyani and His Book Taqyid al-Muhmal

This article introduces the Andalusian hadith master Abu Ali al-Husayn ibn Muhammad ibn Ahmad al-Ghassani al-Jayyani, born in 427 AH and deceased in 498 AH. He studied hadith in al-Andalus without traveling beyond it, began his serious pursuit of knowledge in 444 AH, learned from major hadith authorities, and was praised by more than one later scholar.

Among his most important works is *Taqyid al-Muhmal wa Tamyiz al-Mushkil*, a highly valuable book devoted to narrators in the *Sahihayn*, especially *Sahih al-Bukhari*, whose names, lineages, or identifying marks appear in abbreviated, ambiguous, or confusing form. The work became one of the earliest and most important books in this field, to the point that later scholars were said to depend heavily on it.

The article outlines five major sections of the book: titles and epithets, names that are written alike but pronounced differently, unidentified teachers of al-Bukhari, warnings about errors in *Sahih al-Bukhari*, and warnings about errors in *Sahih Muslim*. In the final two sections, especially those related to al-Bukhari, al-Ghassani made a substantial critical contribution by tracing problems in chains, narrator names, and occasional textual issues.

He states in his own introduction that he intended to mention the errors found in the isnads, most of which he attributes to later transmitters of the books, while clarifying the correct form and identifying teachers whom al-Bukhari cited without full attribution. The article presents this work as a significant continuation and expansion of hadith criticism beyond the earlier efforts of al-Daraqutni.
