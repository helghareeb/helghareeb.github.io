## Cohesion and Organization in the Ranks of Fighting in the Cause of Allah

This article studies the verse, “Indeed Allah loves those who fight in His cause in a
row as though they are a solid structure,” as more than a military image. It treats the
verse as a concentrated Qur'anic lesson in discipline, order, steadfastness, and unity
around a righteous aim.

Drawing on a reading from *Itmam al-Rasf bi-Dhikr ma Hawat-hu Surat al-Saff min
al-Ahkam wa-al-Wasf*, the author explores the linguistic force of *saff* and *bunyān
marsūs*. The first points to arrangement and alignment; the second evokes firmness,
cohesion, and a construction whose parts reinforce one another without gaps.

The article also links this Qur'anic ideal to Prophetic guidance about intention and to
the practical ordering of Muslim ranks. The purpose of fighting “in the cause of Allah”
is not mere bravery, visibility, or gain, but the elevation of Allah's word under a
regime of discipline and sincere obedience.

Its conclusion broadens the lesson beyond combat. The verse becomes a model for every
form of collective Muslim endeavor in which hearts, aims, and roles must come together
with clarity, mutual reinforcement, and principled order.
