## Prophet Jesus in Surat al-Saff

This article presents Prophet Jesus, peace be upon him, through the lens of Surat
al-Saff. It emphasizes the Qur'anic balance of honoring him as one of Allah's noble
messengers while firmly rejecting any claim that raises him above servanthood and
prophethood.

The discussion highlights themes associated with his mention in the surah: his miraculous
origin without a father, the signs granted to him, and the Islamic clarification that he
is “a word” and “a spirit” from Allah in a created, not divine, sense. In this way, the
article responds to theological confusion while preserving reverence for Jesus.

A central focus is the verse in which Jesus gives glad tidings of a messenger to come
after him, named Ahmad. The article treats this as part of the continuity of revelation,
showing that the message of Muhammad, peace and blessings be upon him, stands in
fulfillment rather than contradiction to the line of earlier prophets.

Its closing lesson is both theological and devotional: the correct view of Jesus emerges
from revelation, not polemics, and it calls believers to hold together truthfulness,
clarity, and fairness when addressing long-standing religious misunderstandings.
