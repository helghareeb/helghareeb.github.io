## Strategies of Hypocrisy in Undermining Societies

This article argues that one of the gravest dangers facing the Muslim community is not always an obvious enemy from outside, but an internal adversary that dresses itself in the language of loyalty, religion, and reform while working to hollow the community out from within. For that reason, the Qur'an exposes the hypocrites repeatedly and even devotes an entire surah to uncovering their ways.

The article distinguishes between major hypocrisy, in which a person outwardly displays Islam while inwardly concealing disbelief, and practical hypocrisy, in which faith remains but corrupt traits such as lying, betrayal, and breaking promises appear in conduct. It then turns to the Prophetic period, especially Madinah, where hypocrisy emerged as a socially organized force that spread rumors, provoked tribal tensions, and discouraged people from striving and spending.

Drawing especially from Surat al-Tawbah, the author identifies four recurring strategies of hypocrisy in damaging Muslim society: psychological warfare through rumor and panic, attacks on scholars and leaders to create distrust and institutional vacuum, attempts to destroy public symbols and revive old grievances, and economic sabotage by withholding support and draining communal resources.

The article concludes that hypocrisy is not limited to the past. It continues to appear in politics, media, culture, and economic life through deceptive language and internal corrosion. The proper response is collective awareness, intellectual vigilance, trust-building, support for sincere scholars and reformers, and the cultivation of truthfulness and sincerity in speech and action.
