## Summary of *Al-Mujmalat al-Nafi'at* (5)

This fifth and final article in the series discusses difference: its reality, its kinds,
its rulings, and the ethical and methodological confusion that often surrounds it. The
article argues that not every disagreement is blameworthy, and that proper judgment
depends on distinguishing between legitimate difference and difference driven by whim,
ignorance, or sectarian attachment.

It also tackles the careless use of labels such as *shudhudh* against those who depart
from familiar patterns when following evidence. The article insists that true deviance is
not simply مخالفة a majority or a local convention, but rather opposition to revealed
truth and to the path of the believers.

Throughout, the piece seeks to calm panic around disagreement while also refusing to
romanticize it. Difference is a field that requires knowledge, fairness, discipline, and
clarity about when divergence is tolerated and when it becomes destructive.

As the close of the series, it leaves the reader with a framework: if knowledge, taqlid,
ijtihad, and fatwa are all misunderstood, then conflict spreads; but if they are ordered
correctly, many disputes shrink to their proper size.
