This companion notebook gathers selected spiritual and educational lessons highlighted throughout the series.

## Main lines

- The Qur'an does not merely inform; it forms the reader's stance and response.
- The closing divine names of verses train the heart as much as the legal commands do.
- Certainty in Allah's promise is one of the greatest protections against satanic deception.
- Real taqwa cannot be separated from learning the boundaries of Allah.
- Beneficial knowledge is knowledge that leads to following and surrender.
- Qur'anic stories cultivate patience and steadfastness, not mere information.
- Gratitude for blessing includes using one's blessing to benefit others.
- The ending of al-Baqarah returns the heart to faith, submission, and dependence after long legal instruction.
- Tadabbur trains the student to be fair, disciplined, and restrained in judging others and weighing opinions.
